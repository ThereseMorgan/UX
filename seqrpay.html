Seqrpay

A pre-launch tech startup

Overview

When I was brought on, the startup was still testing market validation and working toward identifying a lean MVP. I had to externalize and focus Seqrpay's vision with branding, content copy and the layout design of a conversion-based website. This site needed to speak to and inform 2 different customer segments, and effectively convey how Seqrpay creates value in a space dominated by large brands.

Problem

How Might We help Seqrpay tell their story so that customers resonate with their mission and product.

Solution

A branded, conversion-driven website that leads to app downloads. We focused their message to speak to their 2 customer segments and externalized their ideas for visability, resulting in them placing in a pitching competition.

01 / Research

Testing Assumptions

After meeting with the founders, we developed a research plan with the goal of testing assumptions, gaining insights and identifying opportunities in the market. We felt that Small Business owners would likely be the early adopters, but to remain focused we looked for a segment that was especially impacted.

Small Business Owners

  • Most willing to try something new
  • Most financially insecure
  • Most adaptable
  • Needs brand awareness
  • Least barrers to entry

Millenial Consumers

  • Digital natives
  • Concerned with social ethics
  • Most likely to be using mPOS already
  • Highest % consumers of small business
  • Trusting of digital platforms

Measuring Success

What we did well

We dived deep! There was a high level of focus on research and analyzing the existing website. We focused on ensuring we were speaking to the right users, and did a lot of research to gain insights on what the Department of Education’s needs and KPIs were.

What I would do differently

There were a lot of UI cooks in this kitchen. I would have narrowed the scope of my contribution, as I believe it wasn’t as efficient or effective touching every on this project. There was also an incident where something I had done overrode someone else’s work. This created a bad feeling and I had to work to rebuild trust. Next time I would make certain that every team member was on board with iterations.
