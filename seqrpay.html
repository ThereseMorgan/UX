Overview

When our design team was brought on, the startup was still testing market validation and not yet focused on a lean MVP. We were tasked with testing their assumptions through customer validation, and delivering a branded style guide and website within 2 weeks.


Problem

How Might We help Seqrpay tell their story so that customers resonate with their mission and product.

Solution

A branded, conversion-driven website that leads to app downloads.
A branded, conversion-driven website that leads to app downloads. We focused their message to speak to their 2 customer segments and externalized their ideas for visibility,

Impact

Customer validation, narrowing and unifying team’s focus for MVP, visuals for investors to latch on to, resulting in them placing in a pitching competition.



Stakeholder Overview

Seqrpay

Seqrpay is a pre-launch startup aiming to compete against big names such as Square, Toast, Paypal and Google/Android Pay. They are a re-imagined POS and mPOS system designed with small business in mind.

We extracted the main colors from the logo to form the color palette, tweaking the bright blue to account for accessibility. High Fidelity Prototype

My final design addressed the user's needs to stay informed and take advantage of available opportunities. Including a more intuitive structure allows users to not get lost in the process, resulting in an improved overall experience navigating the site.


Lessons Learned

Working within a short timeframe with stakeholders created a challenge to get deliveralbes on time. This project made me understand what it means to design mobile-first. While we accounted for responsive design, we spent so much of our energy on the desktop view that I feel other layouts were less resolved.

We also learned the importance of creating meeting recaps to ensure everyone is on the same page with decisions made. I missed a big meeting over one weekend, where a lot of decisions were made. A meeting recap would have helped orient me with the team quickly.
